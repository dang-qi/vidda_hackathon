# Video prompts for the 5-minute presentation

Prompts for **OpenAI Sora 2** (also usable with Runway Gen-4, Veo 3, Kling 2). Each clip is 5–10 seconds — designed to be cut into the live screen recording as opening, transition, and closing B-roll over voiceover.

**Common style anchor** (paste at the end of any prompt for set consistency):
> *Editorial cinematic style, restrained palette of deep navy (#0c1f2f), soft Vidda blue (#0875b7), warm cream and amber accents. Shallow depth of field, soft natural light, slight film grain, no text or letters on screen, no flags, no logos, 24fps.*

Aspect ratio: **16:9, 1920×1080**. Duration: **5–10 s per clip**.

---

## Clip 1 — Cold-open hero (8 s)

**Used at:** 0:00, before the spoken hook. Plays under the title card.

**Prompt:**
> A slow cinematic dolly forward through a vast, dimly lit European bank archive at night. Aisles of grey filing cabinets recede into infinity on both sides. Each drawer is slightly open and overflowing with paper forms. Above the aisles, faint glowing blue and amber regulatory paragraphs float in the air like translucent text fragments — abstract shapes, no readable letters. The camera glides at chest height, soft volumetric haze, a single warm desk lamp visible in the far distance casting a pool of light. Cinematic, painterly, no people in frame, soft ambient drone score implied. Editorial palette of deep navy, soft Vidda blue, amber lamp glow, slight film grain.

**Why it works:** sets the "this problem is too big for one human" mood without literally showing a compliance officer; the floating regulatory glyphs subliminally introduce the EU + national-law motif you reveal later.

---

## Clip 2 — Two-layer architecture reveal (6 s)

**Used at:** ~0:45 transition, after you say "we layer that country's national law on top of the EU core."

**Prompt:**
> A slow camera reveal of a translucent isometric architecture floating in calm grey-cream space. At the base, a large glowing platform shaped like a ring of twelve soft golden stars rotates almost imperceptibly. Three smaller translucent platforms tinted with subtle gradients — Scandinavian blue-and-yellow, Mediterranean red-and-gold, Central European black-red-amber — descend gently from above and stack onto the base in sequence, settling into floating tiers connected by thin vertical beams of light. The camera tilts up slightly as the last layer locks into place. Calm, weightless motion, soft caustics, no text or letters, restrained editorial palette, slight bloom.

**Why it works:** literal visualisation of the EU core + three national overlays; the three layers descending in sequence echoes the "we shipped three, the pattern scales to 27" claim.

---

## Clip 3 — The country swap moment (6 s)

**Used at:** ~2:15, as you click between countries in the demo. Plays as a quick cut to camera, then back to screen.

**Prompt:**
> A close-up of a single glass card floating in soft light. The card holds a translucent silhouette of a desk, a document, and a small abstract emblem. As the camera holds still, the card rapidly cycles through three colour temperatures and ambient lighting setups: first cool Scandinavian blue with hints of yellow, then warm Mediterranean red and gold, then crisp Central European grey with black and amber accents. Each shift takes about 1 second and is accompanied by a subtle whoosh of light. The composition stays identical between shifts — same desk, same posture, same document — only the colour, light direction and emblem change. Cinematic, calm, deliberate, no text, no flags, no letters.

**Why it works:** mirrors the on-screen "click and the matrix re-colors" beat; emphasises "same role, three jurisdictions" through a single composition under three lighting setups.

---

## Clip 4 — Audit-ready close (7 s)

**Used at:** 4:50, under the closing line. Plays as the final shot before the end card.

**Prompt:**
> A slow tilt-down camera move onto a single elegant matte-finish folder resting on a polished walnut desk in a quiet, sunlit regulator's office. The folder cover carries a stylised ring of twelve gold stars, embossed. Three small coloured tab markers protrude from the side — one cool blue, one warm red, one deep gold-on-black. A fountain pen rests across the folder. Soft morning light from a tall window on the left, dust motes drifting through the beam. The camera comes to rest just above the folder; nothing else moves. Calm, authoritative, slightly cinematic, editorial palette of deep navy, walnut brown, cream daylight. No text, no letters, no numbers visible on the folder or pen.

**Why it works:** ends the video on the "audit-ready / regulator-facing" emotional beat; the three coloured tabs echo Sweden/Spain/Germany without literal flags; the fountain pen + folder reads as "ready to sign off" — perfect under the 2027-deadline closing line.

## How to edit them into the demo video

A workable 5-minute structure:

- **0:00–0:08** — Clip 1 plays full-screen under the title and the opening voiceover.
- **0:08–2:15** — Cut to live screen recording of the app (role pick, country pick, agents running, matrix view with Swedish overlay).
- **0:45–0:51** — Optional: brief picture-in-picture of Clip 2 (top-right corner) as you say the "two-layer architecture" line, then back to full screen.
- **2:15–2:21** — Cut to Clip 3 full-screen during the first country swap, then back to live demo for Spain and Germany.
- **2:50–3:55** — Live demo continues: compare view, training plan, audit pack.
- **4:50–4:58** — Cut to Clip 4 under the closing line.
- **4:58–5:00** — End card with logo.

## Notes

- Each clip should be generated at **1080p 16:9**, with **no audio** baked in — score it in the edit.
- If your video tool struggles with the "no text" instruction, regenerate with **"absolutely no text, no letters, no numbers, no flags, no logos visible anywhere in frame"** as the last sentence.
- Keep all four clips in the same generation session if your tool supports it, to anchor the visual style across the set.
